Clubs & Initiatives

Our PTA supports enriching clubs and initiatives that help students explore interests, build skills, and grow in confidence beyond the classroom.

Clubs

Chess Club

How do I join?

Join the WhatsApp group: chat.whatsapp.com/EfNxmY62ZCu4DZ9Usyb42P

When do we meet?

Fridays at 5pm in the school garden (or Gia Gelato if weather is bad). Goal is once a week.

What are the rules during a meetup?

1. Everyone plays. 2. If someone asks you to play them, try to say yes. 3. Set up the board after you finish a game. 4. Don't touch other people's pieces. 5. Be kind.

Can I drop off my kindergartener?

No. Chess Club does not supervise young children independently — they need to be old enough to self-release from CASPER.

How can I help?

Show up, teach kids to play, volunteer to bring snacks, help set up before 4pm, help clean up after. If you know a good indoor spot for winter, that would be great too.

Who's running this?

Jon Huang (no, he's not that good at chess). Contact via the WhatsApp group, 646-360-0320, or chess@jonhuang.com.

Culture Club

Team: Dana Patton and Raquel D'Apice

Mission: To cultivate an environment where individuals are kind, compassionate, and understanding toward one another.

Focused projects include:

  • 2nd Graders & BBBS Mentoring Program — Rocks Projects

  • Champion Readers Program: classroom activities themed around Kindness, Friendship, and Empathy
  • Kindness Challenge, Pennies for Patients Coin Drive, UNICEF, Make-A-Wish, and St. Jude's Children's Hospital collections

To join, email ps5CKT@gmail.com.

Garden Club

Who can join: Middle school grades — no experience needed.

When & where: Every Wednesday, 3–4pm, school garden.

Each week members plant and tend seasonal flowers, vegetables, and herbs; learn about transplanting and composting; do nature-inspired crafts; and explore NJ natives, urban tree canopy, pollinators, birds, and insects.

Music Theatre

The Dr. Michael Conti PS5 Theater Club offers musical theater to students in grades 4–8, culminating in a live performance. Rehearsals led by Ms. Valentin engage students creatively through singing, acting, set design, and costume making.

Initiatives

We support enrichment through art residencies, school assemblies, and programs that promote inclusion, advocacy, and a love of reading.

Art Residencies

Theatre with Speranza Theatre Company (K & 1st)
Students work with Speranza right in their classrooms through theater games and creative writing, expressing their unique voices and building cultural exposure. Speranza is a nonprofit, women-led NJ Theater Alliance member. The PS5 PTA raises funds to support this program — email jerseycityps5pta@gmail.com to join the committee.

Dance with Segunda Quimbamba Folkloric Center (Pre-K)
Pre-K students learn the rhythms, movements, and cultural traditions of Afro-Puerto Rican dance and music, building coordination, confidence, and cultural awareness.

School Assemblies

PS5 hosts a variety of engaging assemblies including:

  • Planetarium, Liberty Science Center (LSC), Rutgers Science Bus
  • Lizard Guy and a magician
  • Sk8 4 Life — wellness and resilience through skateboarding and storytelling
  • Middle school assemblies on anti-racism and internet safety

Book Fair

The PS5 PTA partners with the school for the annual Book Fair, fostering a love of reading and raising classroom funds. Our Books for the Classroom initiative lets families contribute directly to classroom supplies, snacks, and special projects.

PS5 Little Free Library

Located at the main entrance. Bring books for kids — borrow and give freely!

Invited Speakers

The PS5 PTA brings in guest speakers — authors, scientists, artists, and community leaders — to connect classroom subjects to real-world applications and spark curiosity for middle school students.

Parent Workshops

Workshops on internet safety, anti-racism in education, and neurodiversity — supporting an informed, inclusive, and compassionate school community.
